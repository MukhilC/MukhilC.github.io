# Background

Hello there! I am Mukhil C. I am a astrophysicist, currently working on gravitational wave physics. My inspiration/trigger for getting into astrophysics in general was an instance in a documentary on astronauts that I watched when I was in grade school. In the docu, there was an instance when they showed an astronaut floating in empty space with the Earth behind him. That instance wowed me. It told me that they, and in turn we, had gone to places no person would have thought a person could ever go to. Hence, then and there, I decided I would be an astronaut. Alas, life is too cruel (or the doctor handling my birth was), as I had a cut near the pupil of my right eye which caused a significant loss of vision. But, I still wanted to do something with respect to space, the wonder must not die, I thought then. So I chose astrophysics as this would allow me to gain an understanding of how everything, if not all, works. 

And here I am doing research in the field, both under supervision and independently.

# Research

My current research mainly pertains to the dynamics and stability of hierarchical triple systems, especially concerning the Kozai-Lidov mechanism and it's effect on the orbital dynamics. I am also working on simulating the gravitational waveforms produced by these systems. I have submitted a paper on the same, titled "Dynamics of Hierarchical Triple Systems and their Gravitational Wave Detectability", which is currently under review. Check out this [poster](./poster.md)  for more information on the project. This paper is based on my Master's Thesis, titled "Towards Gravitational Wave Templates for Hierarchical Triple Systems: A Preliminary Study" and can be found [here](./thesis.md).

A preprint based on my Master's thesis can be found at [Preprints](https://www.preprints.org/manuscript/202601.1591)
